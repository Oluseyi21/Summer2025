About Polaroid

Polaroid, a name synonymous with instant photography, boasts a rich history of innovation and cultural impact. Founded by Edwin Land in 1937, the company revolutionized photography with its introduction of thee first commercially viable instant camera, the Polaroid Model 95, in 1948. This camera allowed users to capture and develop photos within minutes, a concept that was revolutionary at the time.

Early Days and Birth of Instant Photography

1937: Polaroid Corporation was founded by Edwin Land and George W. Wheelwright III, initially focusing on products related to polarized light.

1947: Land unveiled the first instant fil, demonstrating a one-step, one-minute photographic process.

1948: The Polaroid Model 95, the first commercially available instant camera, debuted, selling out immediately at a department store in Boston.

1950: Black and white instant film became available, replacing the initial sepia-toned prints.
